Evolution of Type

A journey through the milestones that shaped modern typography.

1440
Gutenberg Press
Johannes Gutenberg invents the movable type printing press, revolutionizing the spread of knowledge and democratizing text.
1501
Italic Type
Aldus Manutius introduces italic type, creating a new way to emphasize text and save space in printed books.
1757
Baskerville
John Baskerville creates his eponymous typeface, pushing the boundaries of contrast and refinement in letterforms.
1957
Helvetica
Max Miedinger designs Helvetica, the typeface that would come to define modernism and corporate identity.
1996
Web Fonts
Microsoft releases the Core Fonts for the Web, bringing typographic diversity to the emerging internet.
2016
Variable Fonts
OpenType 1.8 introduces variable fonts, enabling infinite axes of variation within a single font file.
